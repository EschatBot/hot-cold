The tool
What it does
Hot/Cold analyzes conversations — text or live audio — to reveal the invisible social dynamics between people. It reads linguistic patterns, vocal tone, and conversational structure to score every directional relationship: who's warm toward whom, who's checked out, who's performing politeness, and who's genuinely engaged.

Signals
What it reads
🔥
Warmth
Longer replies, questions about the other person, name usage, inclusive language ("we," "us"), future references
❄️
Coldness
Short replies ("k," "fine"), no questions, distancing language, topic avoidance, delayed responses
⚡
Engagement
Fast replies, topic initiation, elaboration, exclamation, laughter reciprocity
😶
Disengagement
Flat pitch, compressed responses, exit language ("anyway…"), decreasing message length
👑
Dominance
Lower pitch, holds the floor longer, others accommodate their vocal patterns, interruption frequency
🎭
Performance
Words say "warm" but tone says "flat" — detecting the gap between what's said and how it's said
🎯
Rapport
Vocal convergence, mirroring, synchronized laughter — speakers unconsciously matching pitch and rate
🚪
Exit Signals
"I should probably…" + pitch flattening + response compression = they want out

Honesty
We don't read minds.
We read signals.

Hot/Cold doesn't claim telepathy. It surfaces patterns that humans already detect subconsciously but can't articulate. Every score is probabilistic, directional, and transparent — you can see exactly which signals drove each reading. Think of it as social radar: it won't tell you what someone is thinking, but it will show you what their behavior is signaling. The difference matters.
